Model Critique

The model does not ask for faith. It asks for verification.

If you have questions, doubts, or objections — here are answers to the most common ones.

"The terms 'Essence,' 'Purpose,' 'Meaning' — this is a subjective choice, not objective truth."

Yes, it is a choice. But not arbitrary.

Any living system — a person, a family, a corporation, a state — in order to exist, must answer three questions:

  • "What am I made of?" — Essence. Without identity and resources, the system disintegrates.
  • "Where am I going?" — Purpose. Without direction, the system is chaotic and vulnerable.
  • "Why do I endure?" — Meaning. Without justification, the system cannot withstand crises.

Try to find a living system that does not answer these three questions. If you find one — we will add a fourth vector. But so far, no such systems exist.

"Why exactly two layers — vital and noetic?"

Because any experience has two sides.

Vital — body, resources, food, survival, money, safety. It is healed through resources.

Noetic — identity, meaning, values, trust, love, truth. It is healed through reflection.

If you mix the layers — you cannot diagnose:

  • Hunger cannot be cured with a lecture on meanings.
  • Emptiness cannot be cured with money.

Two layers are not philosophy. They are practical necessity.

"Why three floors?"

Because any system at any moment is in one of three modes:

  • Floor 1 — reactive, without reflection. Panic, herd instinct, instincts.
  • Floor 2 — role-based, submission to norms. Plans, career, duty.
  • Floor 3 — integral, freedom and choice. Awareness, creativity, acceptance of finitude.

This is not philosophy. This is empirical reality. You can observe these modes in yourself and in others every day.

"This is just another philosophical speculation, there are thousands like it."

The model does not claim to be truth. It claims to be functional.

It is a tool. Like a hammer: it is not "truth," but it drives nails. If someone says a hammer is "subjective" — you just drive a nail and show the result.

The criterion of the model is not "truth," but predictive accuracy and explanatory power.

"Maslow's pyramid, Frankl's logotherapy, Kierkegaard already explained this."

All these models describe different planes of reality.

  • Maslow — hierarchy of needs.
  • Frankl — will to meaning.
  • Kierkegaard — stages of personality.

Our model is the only one that:

  1. Collects all these planes into a single coordinate system.
  2. Works on any scale — from a person to an empire.
  3. Provides a tool for diagnosing ruptures and the figure of the "Thief."

We do not negate our predecessors. We build on them, adding volume.

"You cannot prove that your model is the only correct one."

We are not trying.

The model is not a religion. It does not claim to be the "only correct" path. It claims to be a "working map."

If you have a more accurate map — show it. We will test it. If it is more accurate — we will switch to it. But for now, our map shows ruptures that others do not see. And that is its proof.

Conclusion

The model is not defended. It is used.

If it works — it does not need defense. If it does not work — it needs to be changed.

As long as it works — critics can test it. But to refute it, they will have to refute reality, not the model.

The model is open to change. If you find a system that cannot be described by three vectors, two layers, and three floors — we will revise it. If you show a rupture that the model does not see — we will upgrade it. If you propose a more precise tool — we will switch to it.

This is science, not religion.
